# HR-Attrition-Project
HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)

This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition patterns using an interactive Power BI dashboard. The goal is to help HR teams understand key factors affecting employee turnover and support better workforce decision-making.

🎯 Key KPIs

The dashboard highlights important workforce metrics such as:

Overall Employees – Total number of employees in the organization
Active Employees – Employees currently working in the company
Attrition Count – Total number of employees who left the company
Attrition Rate – Percentage of employees who left compared to the total workforce
Average Age – Average age of employees in the organization

📈 Visualizations Used

The dashboard includes multiple visualizations to analyze attrition trends:

Department-wise Attrition (Pie Chart) – Shows which departments have the highest employee turnover.
Employees by Age Group (Column Chart) – Displays workforce distribution across different age groups.
Job Satisfaction Rating (Matrix) – Analyzes employee satisfaction levels across job roles.
Attrition Rate by Gender (Donut Chart) – Compares attrition trends between male and female employees.
Education-wise Attrition (Bar Chart) – Highlights attrition based on employee education background.

🎛 Interactive Features
Education Slicer – Allows users to filter the dashboard by education level for deeper analysis.

Key Insights:

Department-wise Attrition: The analysis shows that some departments experience higher attrition compared to others, indicating potential issues related to workload, work environment, or career growth opportunities.

Age Group Trend: Most employees fall within the mid-age groups, and attrition tends to be higher in certain age ranges, suggesting that career stage may influence employee turnover.

Job Satisfaction Impact: Employees with lower job satisfaction ratings tend to have higher attrition, highlighting the importance of improving workplace satisfaction and engagement.

Gender-based Attrition: Attrition rates differ slightly between male and female employees, which may indicate differences in job roles, workplace experience, or external factors.

Education-wise Attrition: Attrition patterns vary based on education level, which can help HR teams understand whether certain qualification groups are more likely to leave.




The objective of this dashboard is to provide data-driven insights into employee attrition, enabling HR teams to identify trends and potential factors influencing employee turnover.
